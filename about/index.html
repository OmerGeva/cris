Cristina Alonso Toro

Cristina is an art lawyer and consultant internationally recognized for her expertise in art and culture, with a distinguished background specializing in intellectual property and art law. Her passion for the arts is not only a personal pursuit but a professional calling, guiding her through a career dedicated to the enrichment and appreciation of artistic expression.

Her journey seamlessly weaves together roles within esteemed art institutions and legal practice, creating a unique blend of creative and pragmatic knowledge. This dual expertise ensures that her clients receive comprehensive guidance encompassing both the aesthetic and legal dimensions of art collection and curation.

Her academic credentials have allowed Cristina to transition fluidly from the legal sector to the heart of the art world. This transition has empowered her to advise collectors, galleries, and corporations with a nuanced understanding of both the artistic and legal landscapes. Cristina has advised private collectors on building and managing their collections while staying compliant with global laws and regulations.

Cristina is also actively involved in the technology scene, conducting research at the intersection of art and technology. Her work explores the integration of artificial intelligence and digital art, reflecting her commitment to understanding and shaping the evolving landscape of contemporary art.

In addition, Cristina is deeply engaged with the art community, fostering collaborations that enrich the international cultural landscape. She connects the art worlds from Asia to the Middle East and across Europe, bridging diverse artistic traditions and fostering a global dialogue.

Cristina has pursued her studies in Spain, the United States, Israel, and Italy. She currently lives between Seoul, Dubai and Madrid.
